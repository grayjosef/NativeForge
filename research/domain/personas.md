# Personas

Distilled from source report Section 3. The first three are M0-critical — the demo speaks to them directly. The rest inform M1+ feature priorities.

## 1. Tribal Grant Manager (M0 critical)

The primary user. Manages the entire grants process from opportunity identification through closeout.

- **Top pains:** too many grants vs. too little staff; redundant data entry; missed deadlines; consultant costs; reporting burden; staff turnover with no institutional memory.
- **Key decisions:** which grants to pursue; when to engage tribal council; how to allocate limited staff time.
- **Dashboard needs:** active pursuits by deadline; pipeline stage by grant; upcoming compliance deadlines; tasks assigned to others; alerts for new opportunities matching priorities.
- **Most valuable feature:** reusable entity profile that eliminates per-application data entry.
- **Distrust triggers:** auto-submit without review; AI drafts with inaccurate community statistics; data stored outside tribal control.

## 2. Tribal Administrator (M0 secondary)

Executive oversight. Signs as authorized representative. Approves pursuit decisions.

- **Top pains:** not knowing what's in progress; pulled into compliance crises; finding out about missed deadlines after the fact.
- **Dashboard needs:** executive summary — total active grants, total funding under management, upcoming deadlines at risk, open compliance items.
- **Most valuable feature:** real-time status dashboard and email/SMS alerts for at-risk items.
- **Distrust triggers:** software that increases their workload; anything that requires significant training.

## 3. Tribal Council Member / Leadership Reviewer (M1 priority)

Resolution authorizer. May review narratives before submission.

- **Top pains:** asked to sign resolutions at the last minute; not understanding what's being pursued; feeling disconnected from grant strategy.
- **Most valuable feature:** resolution tracker and approval workflow that requests review at the right time.

## 4. Finance Officer / CFO (M1 priority)

Budget development, drawdown management, 2 CFR 200 compliance, Single Audit prep.

- **Top pains:** tracking expenditures across multiple grants; preparing for Single Audit; managing indirect cost rate documentation.
- **Most valuable feature:** budget tracking with alerts when expenditures approach ceiling.
- **Distrust triggers:** financial data that looks inaccurate; tools that interfere with accounting systems.

## 5. Program Director / Department Head (M1)

Implements grant-funded programs. Provides data for performance reports.

- **Most valuable feature:** milestone/task tracker with automated reminders.

## 6. External Grant Writer / Consultant (M1)

Drafts narratives. Researches opportunities. Manages multiple tribal clients.

- **Top pains:** learning each tribe's history from scratch; managing multiple clients; tracking deadlines across clients.
- **Most valuable feature:** AI-assisted drafting with tribe-specific context loaded from the organizational profile.
- **Distrust triggers:** tools that override their writing style.

## 7. Tribal Nonprofit Executive Director (M1)

Native-led nonprofit, not a tribal government. Several grants for community programs.

- **Top pain:** affordable, simple tools.
- **Most valuable feature:** affordable pricing; ability to demonstrate value to funders.

## 8. Compliance Officer (M2)

Ensures 2 CFR 200 + agency-specific compliance. Monitors subrecipients.

- **Most valuable feature:** compliance matrix auto-generated from NOFO extraction.

## 9. IT / Security Administrator (M0 — sovereignty page audience)

Manages system access, data security, sovereign data controls.

- **Top pains:** uncertainty about what data leaves tribal servers; lack of audit logging in most SaaS tools.
- **Most valuable feature:** audit logs, RBAC, clear documentation of data storage.
- **Distrust triggers:** opaque data handling; AI that may use tribal data for training; lack of clear data residency.

## 10. Auditor / Read-Only Reviewer (M2)

Single Audit, federal site visits, internal audits.

- **Most valuable feature:** clean, date-stamped audit trail with all document versions and approvals preserved.

## Implications for M0

The demo speaks to **personas 1, 2, and 9**. It does not need to speak to 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, or 10 — those are M1+ buyers and users. The temptation to demo finance integration or audit prep in M0 is strong; resist it. The pillar gets diluted.
