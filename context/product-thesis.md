# Product Thesis (one page)

NativeForge is a sovereignty-first grant pursuit and compliance operating system for Native nations and Native-serving organizations.

## The wedge, in one sentence

The product wedge is the gap between **finding a grant** and **submitting a compliant, competitive application** — a gap that no existing product closes for tribal grant recipients in a single workflow built around their sovereignty.

## Why this gap is real

The federal grant software market clusters into discrete lanes:

- Grant discovery (Instrumentl, OpenGrants)
- Grantmaker management (Fluxx, Foundant, Blackbaud)
- Generic nonprofit grant tracking (Submittable, GrantHub)
- Government grant administration (Euna/AmpliFund, SmartSimple)
- Tribal post-award ERP (Arctic IT GovCase, on Microsoft Dynamics)
- Generic AI grant-writing wrappers (Granted AI, Grantable)

None of them combine the eight capabilities a tribal grant manager needs: tribal-specific opportunity ingestion, eligibility scoring, NOFO requirement extraction, form autofill from a reusable entity profile, culturally guarded AI drafting, tribal resolution tracking, post-award compliance, and data sovereignty architecture.

That combination is not a feature. It is a category.

## Why now

- 574 federally recognized tribes in the U.S.; thousands of additional Native-serving organizations.
- $40B in net federal obligations to tribal entities in FY2023, across ~16,000 grants.
- The U.S. government's own June 2024 Tribal CX Pilot documented five critical unmet needs: skilled staff, seamless transitions, offline accommodations, easy portal access, simplified reporting.
- A remote Alaska Native village returned an entire emergency rental assistance grant because quarterly reporting required chartering a plane to find internet. The infrastructure problem is real, but the software problem is solvable.
- AI-native NOFO parsing and drafting are now technically feasible. Two years ago they were not.

## Why us

ContractForge already solves the structurally analogous problem for federal contracts. The engine — opportunity ingestion, scoring, profile-driven autofill, pursuit pipeline — generalizes. NativeForge is not a greenfield build. It is a disciplined extension of an existing engine into a domain where the cultural and sovereignty requirements demand product-level care that a generic competitor cannot retrofit.

## What we will not be

- A consultant replacement. NativeForge is a capacity amplifier; the consultant relationship still has value, and tribes who want to keep theirs should be able to.
- A pan-Indian generalization machine. Every tribe is its own nation. The product reflects that or it fails.
- A vendor that trains AI models on customer data. Sovereignty is in the architecture, not just the marketing.
- An auto-submit tool. Every form, every narrative, every score has a human review gate. The AI does the repetitive parts so the human can focus on the parts only the human can do.

## The disciplined version of the bet

NativeForge is viable. The market gap is real. The wedge is concrete. The existing ContractForge engine gives us a head start no greenfield competitor can match. But the first engineering priority is not features — it is product separation and demo/tenant isolation, so we do not poison customer trust on day one.

Build the walls around the launchpad first. Then build the rocket.
