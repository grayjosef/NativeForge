## Closest competitors and how to position

### Instrumentl

The strongest pre-award competitor for nonprofit grant discovery. No tribal-specific features, no sovereignty architecture, no form autofill, no tribal eligibility tagging. Prices $179/month minimum.

**Position against:** "Instrumentl is built for nonprofit prospect tracking. NativeForge is built for tribal grant pursuit. We score eligibility against your specific entity type, parse NOFOs into checklists, autofill SF-424s from your tribal profile, and protect your sovereignty in our architecture."

### Euna Grants (AmpliFund)

Most visible tribal marketing of any major vendor. But the offering is Euna Grants applied to tribal contexts, not a redesigned product. Post-award focused. No tribal eligibility scoring, no NOFO parsing, no cultural drafting guardrails, no data sovereignty architecture.

**Position against:** "Euna treats tribes as a customer segment. NativeForge treats tribal sovereignty as a design principle."

### Arctic IT GovCase

The only true tribal-first competitor. Microsoft Dynamics-based ERP. Genuine tribal specificity (enrollment, court, social services). But it's post-award financial management, not pre-award pursuit. Requires Microsoft Dynamics expertise. High implementation cost.

**Position against:** "Arctic IT manages your awards after you win them. NativeForge helps you find, evaluate, and win them — and integrates with whatever post-award system you use, including Arctic IT in M1+."

### Granted AI / Grantable

Emerging AI-native grant writing. NOFO parsing, requirement extraction, AI drafting. Closest technical analogs to NativeForge's drafting engine. But no tribal-specific features, no cultural guardrails, no sovereignty architecture, no awareness of tribal-specific grant sources.

**Position against:** "Grantable is generic AI grant writing. NativeForge is grant intelligence built for tribal nations — same AI horsepower, but with eligibility scoring against tribal entity types, source ingestion from BIA/IHS/ANA/CTAS, and cultural guardrails that prevent pan-Indian generalization."

## Market gap (one paragraph)

No existing product combines pre-award grant intelligence + tribal eligibility scoring + AI drafting with cultural guardrails + form autofill + data sovereignty architecture for Native nations and Native-serving organizations. The closest competitive threat would be Euna or Arctic IT acquiring an AI-native grant tool and integrating it with their existing tribal products. As of May 2026, that has not happened.

## What this means for M0

The demo emphasizes the combination, not any one feature. A buyer should walk away with: "this is the tribal grant tool" — not "this is another grant tool that has tribal mode."

The five things a buyer should remember after the demo:

1. The product knows we're a tribe and adjusts everything accordingly.
2. The score is deterministic and explained, not a black box.
3. The sovereignty page is real, not marketing copy.
4. Every AI output is reviewable and overridable.
5. The SF-424 fills itself from a profile we set up once.

If any of those five does not land, the demo is not landing.
